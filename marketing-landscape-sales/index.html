PROBLEM

Sales and marketing: one topic, two perspectives

Forrester asked different cohorts (C-level leaders vs sales and marketing practitioners) about alignment. Those are not two answers to one pooled survey; they are two groups describing what they see.

C-level respondents

82%

said sales and marketing were aligned.

Sales & marketing professionals

35%

reported true alignment in their organisation.

The gap matters: strategy can look “aligned” on paper while day-to-day teams still feel separate systems and unclear impact.

Source: Forrester, “The Truth About B2B Sales And Marketing Alignment” (blog). Self-reported; directional. Verify wording against the primary article before external use.

On customer calls

“Do you feel marketing and sales are aligned?”

82% and 35% are not meant to sum to 100%; they describe different groups. If challenged, open the Forrester post and read how each cohort was defined.

PROBLEM

Three surfaces: ops, execution, AI

A common pattern: marketing ops and tasks live in one place, campaign execution in another, and AI in a third tab. That split makes it harder to learn what works end to end.

Marketing ops

Tasks, planning, approvals, calendars.

Execution

Sends, journeys, assets, reporting.

AI (often siloed)

Research and drafts disconnected from boards and contacts.

Three-surface pattern is a narrative frame for Sales / AMs, not a single third-party %. Pairs with slide 3: sprawl + structure. Sets up monday as one ecosystem.

CRM + MARKETING

Shared contacts, full timeline, triggers that react

80%+

of email marketers saw better results when personalisation used real data (segments, dynamic content), per Litmus survey reporting.

Litmus: The 2024 State of Email Trends. Personalisation needs trustworthy CRM and activity data.

  • Shared contacts board: sales and marketing see the same people and companies.
  • Emails & activities: campaigns visible on the contact timeline, not only in an ESP export.
  • Workflows: board and CRM events can trigger the next campaign step.
  • Collaborative insights: messaging and segmentation insight from the CRM, available to marketing in one place.

AI AT THE CORE

From setup to send, inside the product

monday campaigns builds AI into the workflow: onboarding, brand extraction, campaign creation, and continuous improvement. The roadmap extends that with deeper AI: research drawn from your industry and CRM data, and landing pages generated in-product.

  • Account setup and guided flows.
  • Brand extraction and consistent voice.
  • Campaign creation and optimisation.
  • AI researching for insights from the industry and your CRM.
  • AI building landing pages.
