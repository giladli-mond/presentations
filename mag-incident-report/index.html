Incident Report

Campaigns Email Failure

13 March 2026

A cascading pipeline failure sent ~1.6 million duplicate emails to customers across 35 accounts. One customer, MAG (Motor Auction Group), was severely impacted. The support experience that followed made things significantly worse.

For CPTO and CCO · Prepared by Gilad, Product @ Campaigns

This report covers both the technical incident and the support failure. The support failure is the primary concern: it's a pattern we've seen before (AHA, Feb 2026) and it needs executive attention.

What Happened

The Technical Incident

Friday 13 March
11:00 AM

12:52 PM
System degrades further. Duplicates escalate to 300x
1:25 PM
Incident declared. Initial fix deployed at 1:30 PM, but system still degraded
1:43 PM
MAG sends their campaign. System is degraded. No warning to customers.
3:59 PM

10:41 PM
Six fixes deployed. Sending re-enabled

MAG's campaign to 4,290 contacts resulted in 1,301,446 emails sent (vs 225,000 monthly limit)

The system was known to be degraded when MAG sent their campaign. There was no mechanism to warn customers or prevent sending during an active incident. The 2.5-hour gap between incident declaration and disabling sending is a key finding.

They are considering legal action and switching providers.

The Gap

Nobody was there for the customer

No Account Owner

MAG's account is unassigned. No AM, no CSM. Victoria (previous AM) was covering as a favour. When Fabio flagged the severity at 10:45 PM Friday, she responded at 10:19 AM Monday.

Weekend gap: ~60 hours

Product Team Not Notified

Support handled the crisis for 3+ days without notifying the campaigns product team. The customer found senior monday.com staff through LinkedIn.

No escalation path exists

Support Couldn't Help

Agents gave incorrect information, referenced unrelated tickets, and could not stop the emails. The customer was told to disconnect their own email account and do a "hard refresh".

No emergency tooling

"Monday.com's disaster recovery plan is non-existent and terrible."

Lisa Grimsley, MAG, during follow-up call with Ron (19 March 2026)
Three systemic failures converged: an unassigned account with no one responsible, no mechanism for support to escalate to the product team, and support agents not equipped to handle emergencies. The customer had to find help themselves through LinkedIn. When Fabio (engineering) flagged it to Victoria (previous AM) on Friday night, she had no obligation to respond and didn't until Monday morning.

This Is Not Isolated

The pattern: AHA Advising

AHA Advising (Account 22010338)

Warm prospect referred by the CRM team in March 2025. Never trained on campaigns. Product described as "not usable". Contract terms shifted during migration without communication.

The campaigns product team first learned about AHA through the automated churn analysis, the day after the account had already churned. An 11-month gap.

Two cases in two months.

Both customers received inadequate support during a crisis. In both cases, the product team was never notified. In both cases, the customer had to escalate outside normal channels to get help.

AHA is the pattern evidence. $60K ARR lost because of a combination of contract mishandling, product adoption failure, and support that never escalated to the product team. The product team only found out the day after the churn. Same pattern as MAG: support handles it alone, product team in the dark, customer escalates outside normal channels. Two in two months suggests this is systemic, not incidental.

Actions

What we've done, what needs to change

Done

  • Six technical fixes deployed same day (idempotency guards, timeout adjustments, load shedding)
  • Additional safeguards deployed 18 March (duplicate prevention)
  • Customer call held 19 March with Ron, Victoria, and Gilad
  • Legal consulted (18 March): ToS covers this type of incident. Focus on relationship repair, not compensation framing. Consider commercial goodwill gestures (AI credits, renewal discount)
  • GTM manager (Alessia) informed by product team Friday 10:54 PM. All AMs and AEs briefed by 11:40 PM with action items and customer communication guidance
  • Product team joined all CX escalation channels

Needs Executive Action

  • Escalation path: No mechanism for support to escalate critical product incidents to product teams in real time
  • Severity-1 SLA: No defined SLA for critical customer incidents. This customer waited 3+ days
  • Emergency tooling: Support cannot stop active email sending. Agents gave incorrect guidance because they lack both tools and knowledge
  • Incident safeguards: No mechanism to warn customers or block sending during a known incident
  • Account coverage: Enterprise accounts with no AM or CSM create invisible risk. MAG was unassigned

Pending: Ron connecting with Natalie Margolin · Legal monitoring for PR risk

The technical fixes are solid and were deployed quickly. The systemic gaps are where executive attention is needed. The five items on the right side require decisions and investment that go beyond the campaigns team. The lack of an escalation path between support and product teams is the most critical gap: it's the common thread in both MAG and AHA.
