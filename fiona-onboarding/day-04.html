A note from Gilad

A ritual worth understanding early.

Gilad

One of the things I'm most proud of in this team is how we think about accountability. Not in a "watch your back" way, but in a genuine "I care about this, I said I'd do it, and I want to follow through" kind of way.

Each week, every member of the team shares their Winning Image in Slack, in #campaigns-winning-image. That is the real home of the ritual. Slackbot posts a Monday reminder there, and everyone replies in that thread with what they are focused on for the week.

Read through this page, then join the channel and scroll back through a few Mondays. You'll see exactly how people write theirs. When you're ready to post your own, you'll do it the same way. It is one of the fastest ways to understand what everyone is actually working on.

🏆
◈
The ritual

What is the Winning Image?

Every Monday, Slackbot nudges the channel with the same prompt the team built together:

What is your weekly winning image? Start with the epic, your goal, and why it matters.

Your Winning Image is your answer to that. It is not a long document or a formal plan. It is a clear, personal statement of what you are focused on and what you are trying to complete this week. Think of it as a promise to yourself, made visible to the team.

In practice most people keep it to a handful of bullets: epics, outcomes, or commitments that someone reading on Friday could recognise as done or not done.

How it works

The rhythm.

1
Monday: reply in #campaigns-winning-image
Wait for the Slackbot reminder (Monday morning, UK time). Post your Winning Image as a reply in that thread, not as a standalone message. That keeps everyone's week in one place and makes it easy to scan.
2
Follow the prompt: epic, goal, why it matters
Most people use a short list of bullets tied to epics or outcomes. You do not need a perfect narrative. You do need enough specificity that you (and anyone reading) can tell on Friday whether the week landed.
3
Read everyone else's replies
Same thread, two minutes of reading. You will see who is heads-down, who is on support, and who has capacity. It helps you calibrate asks and offers of help.
4
Friday: mid-week check-in
On Friday afternoon (UK), Slackbot posts another reminder in the same channel asking how your weekly image is going. Use it to share progress, flag a pivot, or say honestly that the week went sideways. Same spirit as Monday: reflection beats performance.
🌿
◈
The most important part

Missing the week is fine.

The Winning Image is not a performance review. Nobody is tracking your completion rate or judging you when life happens, priorities shift, or something unexpected takes the whole week.

What we do care about is the honesty. If you hit it, say so. If you didn't, say that too. The team is not looking for a perfect score. They are looking for someone who is paying attention to their own work and can tell the difference between a week that went to plan and one that didn't.

The most useful thing you can do when you miss a week's image is ask: what did I learn from that? Did I take on too much? Did something interrupt? Was the goal the wrong goal? That reflection is where the real value is. Adjust, and bring a sharper image next Monday.

We do this every week because consistency builds self-awareness. Not because we are perfectionists.

🎯
Specific beats vague
"Finish the analysis" is better than "make progress on data stuff." If it's specific, you'll know on Friday whether you did it.
🪶
Honest beats impressive
Don't write what you think sounds good. Write what you're actually trying to get done. The team will respect that more.
🔄
Adjust, don't abandon
Weeks change. If your image becomes irrelevant by Tuesday, update it. A revised goal is better than a forgotten one.
👀
Read the room
The team's winning images give you real-time context. Who's stretched? Who's focused? You'll know when to reach out and when to give space.
