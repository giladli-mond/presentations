Week 1 in review

A few questions worth sitting with.

No need to answer these out loud or hand them in anywhere. They are just useful prompts before the weekend, and a good basis for your first Winning Image on Monday.

🌱
What landed well?
What made sense faster than you expected? A concept, a conversation, a way the team works. Notice what already feels natural.
🔍
What is still blurry?
Where did you nod along but were not fully sure you followed? Write those down. They are not gaps to be embarrassed about, they are the things to clarify first next week.
🙋
What question have you been holding back?
There is probably at least one. The ones you keep thinking "I should probably ask about that" are usually the most worth asking. Write it down. Then ask it.
⚡
What surprised you?
About the product, the team, the way we work, or any of the sessions this week. Surprises are data. Early impressions are worth capturing before they become assumptions.
🏆
How did your Winning Image for the week land?
Did you hit what you set out to do? Did the week go a different direction? Use the Friday reminder in #campaigns-winning-image to say so. That reflection is the whole point.

Coming next week

Your first real task.

The onboarding task
Exploring the Flywheel

Next week you will start working on your first real onboarding task. It is built around one of the most important concepts in how Campaigns works: the flywheel. The idea of a flywheel is that once it is turning, each part reinforces the next, and momentum builds on itself.

This task is not something we expect you to finish in a week or two. It is designed to be explored and built piece by piece. What matters is that you start, that you ask questions along the way, and that each piece you build teaches you something new about how the product fits together.

What makes this a good onboarding task is the range it covers. You will touch almost every layer of what we do:

💰
Finance and business context
Understanding how the product creates value, how we measure it, and how it connects to the broader business model.
Context
📤
Core product flows
The basic experience of a user sending a campaign, what happens at each step, and how those steps connect.
Product
✨
AI in Campaigns
Where AI sits in the product, what it does today, and where the team is building towards. This is one of our biggest bets.
AI
📊
Data and metrics
What we measure, why those numbers matter, and how analysis connects to decisions. You will get comfortable with this as you go.
Analyst
What to expect

More detail will come in your sessions next week. For now, treat this as a signal: Week 2 has a real north star for you to work towards. One piece at a time, no pressure to have it done quickly.

The best way to prepare is to spend some time this week writing down your biggest open questions about how the product works. Those questions will be your compass when the task starts.
